Trees and Plants of Baner Hill, Pune

Baner Hill is an urban forest reserve in Baner, Pune, Maharashtra, situated at the northern edge of the Western Ghats biodiversity hotspot and the Sahyadri range. The hill's vegetation grows on Deccan basalt and laterite soils at 600–700m altitude, receiving approximately 700–800mm of rainfall annually concentrated in the southwest monsoon (June–September). This creates a distinct ecological profile that supports a specific community of native trees and plants — many of them ecologically significant for birds, insects, and the broader conservation of the Western Ghats urban fringe.

Conservation Value of Baner Hill Trees

Native trees on Baner Hill provide layered ecological services: fruiting trees like Ficus benghalensis, Syzygium cumini, and Phyllanthus emblica support the hill's 88+ acoustically detected bird species. Flowering trees like Butea monosperma and Erythrina variegata are critical for Purple Sunbirds and other nectarivores. Cavity-bearing old trees are essential nesting habitat for hornbills, owlets, barbets, and parakeets. Earth Pulse cross-references this botanical data with live acoustic bird detections to build an evidence base for targeted conservation planting on Baner Hill.

Recommended Trees for Baner Hill

Based on Baner Hill's climate profile (altitude 600–700m, temperature 19–38°C, annual rainfall 700–800mm, laterite/basalt soil, pH 6.5–7), the following species are ecologically optimal and recommended for planting: Ficus benghalensis (Banyan), Ficus religiosa (Peepal), Syzygium cumini (Jamun), Azadirachta indica (Neem), Phyllanthus emblica (Amla), Butea monosperma (Flame of the Forest), Millettia pinnata (Pongamia), Ziziphus mauritiana (Ber/Jujube), Terminalia arjuna (Arjun), Calophyllum inophyllum (Indian Laurel), Dalbergia sissoo (Indian Rosewood), and Bombax ceiba (Silk Cotton). These species support the widest range of birds, insects, and butterflies native to the Sahyadri foothills.
