1. About Earth Pulse

Earth Pulse (earth-pulse.org) is an independent, volunteer-run citizen science environmental observatory monitoring a single location — Baner, Pune, Maharashtra, India (18.5526°N, 73.7819°E) — at the eastern edge of the Western Ghats UNESCO World Heritage ecosystem buffer zone.

The site is operated by an individual researcher and is not affiliated with any government body, university, or commercial organisation. Earth Pulse is provided in good faith as a public resource for education, environmental awareness, and open citizen science.

3. Data and content

3.1 Accuracy and reliability

Earth Pulse displays environmental data collected from automated sensors, third-party APIs, and acoustic AI systems. While we make every reasonable effort to ensure accuracy, we cannot guarantee that all data is error-free, complete, or up to date. Sensor outages, API failures, and model limitations can affect readings.

Earth Pulse data should not be used as the sole basis for decisions affecting health, safety, agriculture, or emergency response. Always consult official sources (IMD, MPCB, CPCB) for authoritative weather and air quality information.
