Earth Pulse is a ground-level environmental observatory on the edge of the Western Ghats — tracking air quality, hyperlocal weather, sound, and biodiversity from one location, continuously, since January 2022.

The objective

To build the most detailed, long-term, freely accessible environmental record of a single location in urban India — combining air quality, hyperlocal microclimate, acoustic biodiversity, and visual documentation into one continuous, open dataset.

Most environmental data is regional and aggregated. Earth Pulse is hyperlocal and hourly. The goal is to reveal patterns that regional data cannot — the morning AQI spike before traffic, the exact temperature when the first monsoon cloud arrives, the dawn chorus shifting as seasons change.

Over time this becomes more than data. It becomes memory, evidence, and a foundation for understanding how a place changes — accessible not just to researchers, but to anyone curious about the world around them.

How it works

Methodology

Weather Union ZWL008983 Google Air Quality API Open-Meteo CAMS Copernicus BirdNET AI v2.4 SDS011 PM2.5 · Phase 2 NDSI Soundscape · Phase 2

Hyperlocal weather

Since October 2025, temperature, humidity, wind, and rainfall are sourced from Weather Union station ZWL008983 — a physical AWS sensor 2.0 km from Baner. Data is logged every 15 minutes. Before October 2025, Open-Meteo ECMWF model data provides the historical baseline.

📡

Why this matters: Regional weather models can be off by 3–4°C for microclimates at the Western Ghats edge. A physical sensor 2.0 km away is significantly more accurate for Baner's conditions.

Air quality

Hourly AQI is sourced from the Copernicus Atmosphere Monitoring Service (CAMS) via Open-Meteo — the same dataset used by European environmental agencies. PM2.5, PM10, NO₂, ozone, CO, SO₂, and ammonia. India AQI from the Google Air Quality API using India's national 0–500 scale. Both stored side by side every hour since January 2022.

Acoustic bird detection

BirdNET v2.4 — neural network by Cornell Lab of Ornithology — runs continuously on a Raspberry Pi facing the Sahyadri foothills. Each detection is logged with its confidence score and matched to the climate reading at that exact hour, enabling correlations between air quality, weather, and bird activity. 228 species predicted for our coordinates this season.

Soundscape index · Phase 2

The Normalised Difference Soundscape Index (NDSI) measures ecosystem acoustic health — the ratio of natural sounds (birds, insects, rain) to human noise (traffic, construction). A second Pi on the south balcony will compute NDSI every 30 minutes, providing a continuous ecosystem health signal for Baner Hill.

PM2.5 sensor · Phase 2

An SDS011 laser particle sensor on the south balcony will measure hyperlocal PM2.5 and PM10 directly at the site — complementing the satellite-derived CAMS data with a ground-truth physical measurement. At 1µg/m³ resolution, it can detect localised pollution events that regional models miss.

Data quality

Gaps from power outages are automatically backfilled on the next run using archive APIs. The database uses upsert logic — re-running any script never creates duplicates. All data stored in UTC, displayed in IST. The combined_readings view merges Weather Union and Open-Meteo seamlessly, with WU taking priority where available.

The hardware & software

Built from scratch, on a balcony

Three Raspberry Pis, a USB microphone, a particle sensor, and a lot of Python. Total hardware cost under ₹12,000. Monthly running cost: ₹40 in electricity.

What comes next

Roadmap

Earth Pulse is a long-term project. Each phase adds a new dimension of measurement.

✓ Complete
Phase 1
Air quality, climate & acoustic monitoring
37,500+ hourly climate records from January 2022. BirdNET acoustic detection live. Google AQI + CAMS data pipeline. Website live at earth-pulse.org. Weather Union hyperlocal integration (208K readings). 48+ bird species detected at Baner Hill.
In progress
Phase 2
South balcony node — second mic, PM2.5 sensor, timelapse
A second Pi 3B on the south-facing balcony directly facing the Sahyadri hills. Second BirdNET microphone, SDS011 PM2.5 sensor, Pi Camera for 3-hour timelapse, NDSI soundscape index every 30 minutes.
Planned
Phase 3
Baner Hill Bioacoustic Index + Bird Species Radar
A composite acoustic health score for Baner Hill, updated daily. Species arrival and departure tracking across seasons — building a phenology calendar unique to this location.
Planned
Phase 4
Baner Lake water quality node
Solar-powered Pi Zero 2W at Baner Lake measuring water temperature, turbidity, and dissolved oxygen — the first multi-ecosystem environmental record linking air, land, and water quality for the Baner area.
Planned
Phase 5
Community node network + public research API
Open the platform for community members to connect their own Pi nodes across Pune. Public research API for academic collaboration. The goal: the most detailed multi-point urban biodiversity dataset in India.
