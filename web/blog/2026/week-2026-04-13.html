🌳 Identified on Baner Hill
Karanj
Pongamia pinnata
Fabaceae
Karanj is a fast-growing nitrogen-fixing tree native to coastal and riverine habitats of the Indian subcontinent, and a characteristic species of the Western Ghats foothills. Its dense flowering attracts pollinators that support the insect prey base for Baner Hill's insectivorous birds.
💡
Karanj seed oil contains karanjin — a natural insecticide used in traditional Deccan agriculture for centuries before synthetic pesticides existed. The same oil is now being studied as a biofuel feedstock.
