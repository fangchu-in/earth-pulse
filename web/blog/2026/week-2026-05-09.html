Our acoustic monitors never sleep. While Pune goes about its day, thousands of kilometres away, birds are already preparing their journey toward Baner Hill — a quiet stopover on one of the world's great flyways. Earth Pulse has been listening since 2022. This is what we've heard.

The Central Asian Flyway passes through Pune

Baner Hill sits at 560m on the edge of the Sahyadri — the Western Ghats. Every year, millions of birds use the Central Asian Flyway, one of the world's great bird highways, passing through Pune as they travel between breeding grounds in Central Asia, Siberia, and Russia, and wintering areas across South Asia.

Most people in Baner don't know this is happening above their heads. Earth Pulse's BirdNET AI hears them by their calls — at 5am, in the dark, before the city wakes.

What the data tells us about migration at Baner

🌅
Dawn is migration hour
Migratory species are detected most between 5:00–8:00am IST. Birds fly overnight and call at dawn to re-orient after landing.
🌧️
Clean air = more detections
On low-AQI days after rain (AQI <40), Earth Pulse logs noticeably more migratory detections at Baner.
📅
Two migration windows
Northward passage in March–April. Southward passage in October–November. Monsoon brings Western Ghats breeding visitors.

Questions about migratory birds at Baner

Which migratory birds visit Baner Hill, Pune?
Earth Pulse has acoustically detected 4 migratory species at Baner Hill including the Clamorous Reed Warbler, Greenish Warbler, Red-breasted Flycatcher, White Wagtail, Common Cuckoo, Barn Swallow, Asian Bee-eater, and Bay-backed Shrike. These birds travel the Central Asian Flyway, passing through Pune between their breeding grounds in Siberia and Central Asia and their wintering areas in South Asia.
When is the best time to hear migratory birds in Baner?
Migratory birds at Baner Hill are most vocal between 5:00am and 8:00am IST — the dawn chorus. Birds fly overnight and call at dawn to re-orient after landing. The best months for migratory species at Baner are October–February (winter visitors from Central Asia) and March–April (northward pre-monsoon passage).
What is the Central Asian Flyway?
The Central Asian Flyway is one of the world's major bird migration routes, stretching from breeding grounds in Siberia, Russia, and Central Asia southward through the Indian subcontinent to wintering areas in South and Southeast Asia. Baner Hill, Pune — at 560m on the Western Ghats edge — sits directly on this route, making it a natural stopover for long-distance migrants.
