Baner sits at the eastern edge of the Western Ghats, where the Arabian Sea branch of the monsoon makes its first landfall over the Sahyadri range. The hills act as a rain shadow catcher — most of the annual rainfall arrives in a concentrated 4-month window from June to September.

What the data tells us
Fun fact: The India Meteorological Department (IMD) defines monsoon onset over Pune when sustained rainfall begins along the Konkan coast and advances inland. Baner's hyperlocal data often shows the first drops 1–2 days ahead of official IMD declarations for Pune city.

Frequently asked questions

When does the monsoon arrive in Baner, Pune?

Based on Earth Pulse data since 2022, the first significant monsoon rain in Baner typically arrives between June 5–15. The official IMD onset over the Konkan coast usually precedes Baner by 2–4 days. The First Rain section above shows the exact first-rain date for every year on record.

How much rain does Baner get annually?

Baner's measured annual rainfall averages around 600–750 mm based on our 4+ year dataset. Nearly 85% of this falls during the June–September monsoon season. The year-by-year chart above shows the full breakdown by month for every year since 2022.

Does rain improve air quality in Pune?

Yes — our data shows the EU AQI in Baner drops measurably within 1 hour of significant rainfall. Heavy monsoon rain produces the largest AQI improvements. The Air Quality & Rain section above shows the exact before/during/after AQI numbers from all rain events since 2022.

How do birds in Baner respond to rain?

Most bird species reduce acoustic activity during rain — heavy rainfall masks their calls and many birds shelter. However, the hour after rain sees a significant surge in bird detections, with many species resuming calls, foraging, and territorial singing. The post-rain dawn chorus during monsoon is one of the most biodiverse soundscapes recorded at Baner Hill.

Where does this rain data come from?

Historical data (Jan 2022 – Sep 2025) comes from Open-Meteo's ECMWF model, recorded hourly to Supabase by our Raspberry Pi at Baner. From October 2025, data is supplemented by Weather Union station ZWL008983, a hyperlocal sensor 2.0km from Baner Hill. Both are merged in a combined_readings view with WU data taking priority where available.
